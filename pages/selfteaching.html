I am mainly self-taught, and sometimes i'm asked where to learn the kind of things i do. So here it is a curated list of didactical devices:


BEGINNER
  • Learning Processing is a great book for starting
  • Adafruit Arduino Tutorials for learning Arduino
  • FORM+CODE a great compendium of generative art

ADVANCED
  • the ofBook is a great resource for openFrameworks
  • Nature of Code is a classic
  • Generative Design is another classic
  • The Book Of Shaders will introduce you to shader alchemy
  • Musimathics for the mathematical principles behind music

LINUX
  • the Arch Wiki is one of the greatest resources ever, so i advice to install Archlinux at least once in your life just to learn how things works from scratch
  • r/unixporn: the home for *NIX customization

DSP
  • Designing Audio Effect Plug-Ins in C++ for dsp basics and sound processing
  • Designing Software Synthesizer Plug-Ins in C++ for sound generation and more advanced filters
  • the KVR Forum, DSP Developement Sect.
  • musicdsp.org archive

ASEMIC WRITING
  • general wikipedia article
  • Adrian Frutiger - Signs and symbols: Their Design and Meaning
